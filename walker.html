Energy Generator (Walker)

Designation: EG-Series (Energy Generators)

Nickname: Walker

Purpose

Portable power stations designed to supply energy to remote or inaccessible sites. Equipped with locomotion to move autonomously across rugged terrain, reducing the need for transport bots.

Capabilities

  • Locomotion: Four-legged walkers with a low, stable profile for climbing rocky or uneven surfaces. AI-assisted pathfinding for obstacle navigation.
  • Energy Production: Fusion or quantum battery technology for continuous power generation. Output connectors for various voltage requirements.
  • Toughness: Reinforced casing to protect sensitive components from harsh environments.
  • Communication: Networked AI for real-time coordination with other assets and centralized control.

Size and Appearance

  • Height: Approximately eight feet tall when walking, with extendable pylons for stabilization when stationary.
  • Appearance: A squat, robust design with a glowing energy core visible through a protective casing.
  • Colors: Standard corporate black with hazard yellow highlights.

History of Development

Originally designed for remote mining sites and asteroid bases, the EG-Series was adapted for planetary exploration. Early prototypes lacked locomotion, requiring manual transport, but the addition of autonomous mobility significantly increased deployment efficiency.
