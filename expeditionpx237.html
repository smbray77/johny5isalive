Expedition PX-237

Mission Overview

Expedition PX-237 was initiated to investigate unexplained energy signatures on a newly discovered planet, evaluate potential resources, and test interdimensional portal technology.

  • Primary Goals:
    • Analyze and secure the unknown energy source for potential exploitation.
    • Map the terrain and establish a foothold for future operations.
    • Evaluate the long-term potential of PX-237 for resource extraction and corporate expansion.

Planning and Execution

  • Pre-Mission Concerns:
    • Corporate prioritization of profit led to cutting corners in safety and materials.
    • Warnings about the ship’s design and rushed development were dismissed.
  • Crew Hierarchy:
    • Upper Class: Corporate overseers and project leads.
    • Middle Class: Scientists, engineers, and AI technicians.
    • Lower Class: Maintenance workers and expendable personnel.
    • Class divides caused internal tension during the mission.
  • Vessel Design:
    • Drive Section: Nuclear-powered antimatter engine for propulsion.
    • Landing Section: Equipped for planetary landing, exploration, and portal deployment.
  • Catastrophic Separation:
    • Faulty explosive bolts caused incomplete separation of the ship's sections.
    • The drive section was flung toward the planet, resulting in a nuclear explosion followed by an antimatter detonation.
    • The landing section, though damaged, continued its programmed descent and activated the portal.

Portal Malfunction and Consequences

  • Initial Issues:
    • PX-237’s magic field and radiation influenced the portal, causing severe malfunctions.
    • Early anomalies included duplication, fusion of tech and biological mass, and corrupted data.
  • Unintended Results:
    • Assets sent through the portal became grotesque hybrids, later known as Unknown Monsters (UM).
    • Aeternum HQ misinterpreted these monsters as native lifeforms, failing to connect them to their own technology.

At-Home Portal Operations

  • Preparation:
    • Extensive testing of portal technology in controlled environments before deployment.
    • Calibration for PX-237’s conditions using limited data from the landing section.
  • Asset Deployment Plan:
    • Initial assets focused on reconnaissance and infrastructure.
    • Later deployments included security measures and experimental technology.
  • Deployment Philosophy:
    • A phased approach prioritized caution but quickly escalated as confidence in the portal grew.
    • Early anomalies and duplication errors were dismissed as minor.
